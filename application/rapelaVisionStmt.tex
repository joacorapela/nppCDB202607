\section{Research Vision}

My research motto is: \textit{Mathematics without biology or biology without
mathematics are not as wonderful}.

Since pursuing my PhD, my research career has been highly interdisciplinary
spanning beautiful analytical (e.g., mathematics, signal processing,
statistics, machine learning, computer science) and neuroscience experimental
subjects (e.g., statistical analysis of responses of single cells,
electrocorticography, Utah arrays, Neuropixels recordings, close-loop
experimental control). I delight when I create a new method to process
biological data, but I delight much further when I apply this method and I
steal a secret from nature.

My \emph{Statistical Neuroscience Research Group} will create advanced
statistical methods to address important neuroscience problems, and collaborate
with Neuroscience experimental groups applying these methods, or methods
created by others, to characterise behavioral and neural data, offline and in
real time, for short- and long-duration experiments.

\subsection{Advanced Statistical Methods}

\subsubsection{Neural Latent Variables}

\paragraph{Offline Inference}

It is challenging to extract meaning from the large number of spikes generated
by high-channel count recordings probes, e.g., Neuropixels. To address this
problem, my supervisor at the Gatsby Unit, Prof.~Maneesh Sahani, and
collaborators, created Gaussian Process Factor
Analysis~\citep[GPFA][]{yuEtAl09}, a statistical method to extract
low-dimensional representation (i.e., latent variables) from high-dimensional
electrophysiological recordings. At the Unit I have distributed a Python
implementation of an extension of GPFA, Sparse Variational Gaussian Process
Factor Analysis~\citep[svGPFA][]{dunckerAndSahani18}, which uses a sparse
variational method, which significantly reduce the memory and time complexity
in the estimation of GPFA models and, criticially, allows point-process
observation, eliminating the need of binning spikes. I have improved svGPFA
(e.g., using the Cholesky decomposition to represent the posterior covariance
matrix), and used it to
\href{https://joacorapela.github.io/svGPFA/auto_examples/index.html}{characterize
neural recordings}.

In Neuropixels recordings from the striatum during sleep, after mice performed
a motor sequence task~\citep{thompsonEtAl22}, I am observing that neural
latents replay patterns previously displayed during behavior. I am not yet
100\% sure that this is a real finding, but if it is the case, it will be the
first time that replay is identified using continuous latent variables of
neural population activity, which could have important consequences in memory
research

My group at NPP will extend svGPFA further. For instance, we will allow svGPFA
to accept external inputs, currently missing in the model. We will also
collaborate with experimental groups at NPP and CDB to investigate replay in
the hippocampus (at the population level and without estimating place fields).
