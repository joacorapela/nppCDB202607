I have followed a non-traditional career path in computational neuroscience.
Following a PhD and two postdoctoral fellowships at the interface of
statistical machine learning and neuroscience—resulting in key publications in
leading journals such as \textit{Neural Computation}, \textit{Network:
Computation in Neural Systems}, and the \textit{Journal of Vision}—I chose to
embed myself within a world-class computational/experimental environment. I
joined the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit and the SWC as a Research
Engineer Fellow to further develop my skills in statistical machine learning,
software engineering and team science.

I have grown enormously at the Gatsby and SWC.
%
At the Unit, I have substantially increased my expertise in signal processing
and machine learning, specially through my weekly meetings with Prof.~Maneesh
Sahani for more than seven years.
%
Alongside developing professional software engineering capabilities, I have
been an active contributor to the institute-wide team science Aeon project.
%
My leadership and research contributions include securing a £800,000 BBSRC
grant as well as co-authoring a \href{}{joint application} between the
Gatsby Unit, the SWC, and the Allen Institute for Neural Dynamics for a
BBSRC/NSF-Bio award.
%
Over the past three years, I have successfully managed a small team of research
software engineers, designed and taught the core Statistical Neuroscience
course for SWC PhD candidates, and mentored two undergraduate fellows through
year-long Simons Foundation fellowships.

While my primary focus during this period has been on software infrastructure
and machine learning training, my next career phase will centre on publishing
research articles, teaching and mentoring students, and establishing active
collaborations with experimental groups at NPP and CDB.
%
I am well prepared for this transition; over the last seven years, I have
developed several research projects that will yield high-quality publications
in the near future. Below, I outline a few of these upcoming papers:

\begin{itemize}

    \item\textbf{Traveling waves during rhythmic speech production:} Expanding
        on single-subject findings reported in my earlier work (Rapela 2016,
        2017, 2018), I am leveraging a newly released dataset across six
        subjects to validate these spatio-temporal dynamics in a larger
        population.

    \item\textbf{Statistical and software foundations of svGPFA:} I am
        preparing a pair of manuscripts documenting the unique numerical,
        statistical, and software engineering features of our Python
        distribution of svGPFA. These will be accompanied by
        a scientific paper demonstrating svGPFA findings on
        interesting neuroscience problems, such as detecting replay in the
        striatum.

    \item\textbf{Algorithmic extensions to svGPFA:} To broaden the scope of the
        framework, I am extending svGPFA to incorporate external inputs and
        support online operation.

    \item \textbf{Bonsai.ML and high-throughput behavioural analytics:} I will
        publish a methodology paper introducing the \textit{Bonsai.ML} software
        package for intelligent, closed-loop experimental control.
        Additionally, in collaboration with an undergraduate mentee, we are
        preparing a research manuscript for publication analyzing the
        statistical properties of long-duration, continual mouse behavioural
        data within the \textit{Aeon} platform.

    \item\textbf{State-space models for kinematics inference:} To address the
        failure of deep pose-estimation networks to account for temporal
        continuity, we have developed
        \href{https://joacorapela.github.io/ssm/auto_examples/index.html#kinematics-inference}{state-space
        models that explicitly infer kinematics}. I will describe these methods
        and demonstrate their advantages in robustly capturing single- and
        multi-animal behaviour.

\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Evidence of productive cross-disciplinary collaborations,
specifically spanning computational or AI methods and neuroscience or
biological systems, for example joint A/I publications, grants, or

I owe much of my research trajectory in computational neuroscience to close
partnerships with experimental collaborators:

\begin{itemize}

    \item During my PhD, I collaborated with Dr.~John Touryan and Dr.~Gidon
        Felsen (UC Berkeley, Yang Dan lab) to characterise responses to natural
        images in the cat primary visual cortex, utilising multivariate
        nonlinear regression models that I developed
        \citep{rapelaEtAl06,rapelaEtAl10}.

    \item During my first postdoc at UC San Diego, a collaboration with
        Prof.~Edward Chang (UCSF) helped me secure my first seed grant. His lab
        generously shared human ECoG recordings from epileptic subjects, which
        I used to fit and evaluate new ensemble dynamical models
        \citep{rapelaEtAlInPrepEDMs}. This rich dataset ultimately enabled me
        to discover travelling waves in human language regions during speech
        production
        \citep{rapelaInPrepTWsInSpeech,rapelaInPrepSyncTWs,rapelaInPrepSyncTWsII}.

    \item Also at UC San Diego, I collaborated with EEG experimentalists
        Prof.~Jeanne Townsend and Dr.~Marissa Westerfield to study the
        relationship between single-trial phase coherence and attentional
        shifts in humans \citep{rapelaEtAl18-avshift}. The machine learning
        innovation in this work was the use of variational inference to
        estimate large-dimensional linear regression models from limited data.

    % \item I was recruited to the Gatsby Unit specifically to collaborate with SWC experimental neuroscientists on advanced statistical data analysis.  One such partnership, with Ms.~Mitra Javadzadeh (Hofer lab), used linear dynamical system models I developed to map long-range cortical communication. She delivered an oral presentation of our findings at CoSyNe 2022.\footnote{\href{https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lG1Rn4AwmY8}{Mitra presented our results at CoSyNe 2022; presentation begins at 2:59:00.}}

    \item At the SWC and Gatsby Unit, I also contributed statistical expertise
        to the analysis of high-throughput behavioural data within the Aeon
        platform \citep{campagnerEtAl25b}. Through this initiative, I partnered
        with Dr.~Goncalo Lopes (Director of NeuroGEARS), with whom I co-secured
        and developed the BBSRC-funded project
        \href{https://gtr.ukri.org/projects?ref=BB\%2FW019132\%2F1}{Machine
        Intelligence for Neuroscience Experimental Control}.

\end{itemize}
